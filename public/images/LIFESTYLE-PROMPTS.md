# Lifestyle photo prompts — "dog next to its own framed portrait"

Goal: candid, real-home photos where the **finished framed portrait** sits in a room and the
**real dog** stands/sits next to it — looking like the owner snapped it on their phone, not a studio shot.

## How to use (important)

1. **Feed the portrait in as an image input.** In Nano Banana Pro / Gemini, attach the generated
   portrait PNG (e.g. Teddy / Biscuit / David) and tell it to keep the artwork inside the frame
   **identical**. Don't describe the art — give it the actual image.
2. **Even better: attach a 2nd image** — a real photo of the same dog. Then the live dog in the
   scene matches the portrait. Nano Banana Pro takes multiple image inputs, so give it both
   (portrait + real dog photo).
3. **Aspect ratio:** 3:2 or 4:5. For the **hero**, ask for negative space (empty wall) on one side
   for headline text.
4. Keep the look **candid**: phone photo, natural window light, slightly imperfect, authentic — the
   opposite of a glossy catalogue render.

## Master template (swap the [brackets])

> Using the attached illustrated pet portrait, create a realistic, candid smartphone photo of that
> exact portrait printed and placed in a thin [black / light-oak] frame with glass, [leaning on the
> floor against a wall / hanging on the wall above a console table]. Next to the frame, a real
> [breed + colour, e.g. brindle chihuahua] dog [sits / stands] [looking at the camera / glancing up
> at the picture]. Setting: a cosy real home — [wooden floor, soft off-white wall, a trailing plant,
> a bit of everyday clutter]. Soft natural window light, warm and homely, shallow depth of field,
> shot on a phone, authentic UGC feel, slightly casual framing. Keep the artwork inside the frame
> exactly as in the attached image — do not redraw or restyle it. No added text, no watermark, no
> extra frames or pictures. 4:5.

## Which website slots these fill

- **hero.jpg** (most important): use a wide 3:2 with the framed portrait on a styled wall and the
  dog beside it, lots of empty wall on the LEFT for the headline. Calm, premium, sunlit.
- **Social-proof / "real customers" row**: the casual, slightly messy versions above — they sell the
  "this is what you'll actually get" story.
- **product-framed.jpg**: the same shot, tighter on the frame, dog optional.

## Variations to try
- "leaning on the floor against the wall" reads more casual/premium than "hanging".
- Add "a hand just out of frame" or "a mug on the table" for extra candid realism.
- For mugs/cases later: same idea — attach the portrait, ask for the print on a white mug on a
  kitchen counter with the real dog blurred in the background.

## Negative prompt (append if your tool supports it)
> no added text, no watermark, no logo, no extra frames or pictures on the wall, do not redraw or
> restyle the artwork inside the frame, no studio-white background, no cartoon, no distorted dog
> anatomy, no duplicate dogs.
